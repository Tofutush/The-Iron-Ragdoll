Sky was just as delighted as Snowstorm when he heard about how we got invited to the Westavian Embassy. He turned Sugarcane's invitation letter over and over in his hands, clutching it tightly, as if it would disappear into thin air the next second.

I had my letter in my hand. When I first received the envelope with my name written in crayon, I had thought it was a mass-printed invitation with Jelly's own marking on top. Instead, it turned out that Jelly drew the entire thing. I could feel the texture of the crayon on the paper if I run my hand over it. It had a blue sky with white clouds, and a single seven-point star where the sun usually hung. A building, presumably the Westavian Embassy, stood on the green ground, and two of what I could only describe as blobs stood next to it. Only the colors of the blobs, blue and purple, suggested that they were supposed to be me and Jelly. The words, if written in their correct spelling, would be "Welcome to the Cultural Bridge Day!"

"I had been keeping tabs on this event, you know," Sky said. "It's the first of its kind, and Checkers is the main organizer, being the cultural attaché after all. It's an event for Westavian Bauhinians to learn more about Westavian culture."

"That sounds like it has nothing to do with us?" Sugarcane said.

"If he told Jelly to invite her friends, then we can best-faith assume he's glad she's making friends."

"Worst-faith, then?" I asked.

Sky gave me a wink. "I like the way you think, but I don't want to worry you too much. Just assume he's happy his daughter is making friends and want to make you feel welcome."
