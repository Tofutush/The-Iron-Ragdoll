Jewel poked her head in the tunnel. "It's awfully dark in there," she said. "Harmony? Can we get your flashlights?"

"Sure," I said. There wasn't enough space for me to set my bag down at ease, so I asked, "Sugarcane, can you open my bag and take out the flashlights?"

Except it was Sunny who answered. "Okay, let me."

No, I didn't ask *him* to do it! I asked for Sugarcane! But Sunny was already unzipping my bag and rummaging through it.

"Got them," he said as he pulled out all three and zipped my bag back up.

The cave was quite deep, though I was pretty sure I heard a couple bats flying overhead. Jewel strode at the lead, flashlight in hand, until the tunnel opened up into a cave, with a single clipboard on the ground and a pen attached to it.

Jewel pounced at the clipboard like a lion at prey, nearly dropping my flashlight on the ground, but Sugarcane caught it last second and handed it to me as Jewel busily signed her name.

"Here," she passed the clipboard to Sugarcane when she was done. Sugarcane passed it to me, and as I signed, I saw that there were still fewer than twenty names on there. A lot of people would have to complete this next year.

I passed the clipboard to Sunny, who passed it to Cosmo, who wondered out loud, "I wonder what would happen if we simply just stole this."

"Cosmo, there are cameras and microphones in here," Jewel said.

"Can the cameras even catch anything, when it's so dark?" Cosmo said.

"The microphones sure can," Jewel snapped back. "We all got our names? Let's get the fuck out of here."

We filed out of the cave, back into the sunlight. "What time is it?" I asked Jewel.

"Three," she replied.

"What happens if we don't get back on time?"

"I don't want to find out," Jewel rubbed her temples and shook her head. "Sorry, but I *really* don't want to be here for a third time."

"I get it," Sugarcane said. "Let's get going, then."

I couldn't bring myself to be mad at Jewel's irritation, because she had been such a great help to us, and I couldn't imagine doing this assignment again next year. But it was still jarring to see her being so visibly annoyed at us, and Sugarcane comforting her, instead of snapping at her, like she would've done with Rose and her disciples.

We made a beeline for the south, Jewel leading the way with the watch trick Sugarcane taught her. There were a *lot* more fallen branches on the ground.

"It must be a nightmare to clean these up," Sunny said, looking at the branches.

"Who cares?" Jewel said.

It occurred to me that Jewel didn't need us at this point. She had the watch.
