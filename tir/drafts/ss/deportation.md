Jelly was rolling along a pink suitcase decorated with magenta ants along a trail.

Jelly looked… mature. Despite still being shorter, now that I knew she was actually my big sister, she started to radiate maturity.

She seemed surprised to see me as I ran over and wrapped her into an embrace.

"Harmony! What are you doing here?"

"I… I came to see you off."

"Oh, okay. I guess we have some time. What could you possibly have to say?"

"I'll miss you."

Jelly sighed. "I'll miss you too, Harmony."

I took the slip of paper out of my pocket. "Here is my email address. Write sometime?"

Jelly took the piece of crumpled paper and flattened it. "I don't have an email though, and my dad wouldn't let me use his computer."

"My mom doesn't, either," I said, reveling in our similarities. "Try pestering him. And there are apps to write emails on your phone, too!"

"I'll keep that in mind," Jelly smiled a little. "Anything else?"

I took a deep breath, and held Jelly's hands in mine. "I wish our countries weren't at rivalry. I wish we could be friends without all the adults meddling. I wish these fake borders and Iron Curtains didn't become real barriers to our friendship!"

Jelly frowned, and snatched her hands out of my grasp. "That's easy for you to say. You're Bauhinian."

"What?"

"See?" She said. "Tell me, in this perfect, borderless world you're imagining, are there any Westavians in there? Or is everyone Bauhinian?"
