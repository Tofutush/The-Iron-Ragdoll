The string broke under both of our weight and we landed hard on the ground. I scrambled up and glanced around for Jelly, but she was nowhere to be found. There wasn't even any footprints on the dirt, let alone the pavement.

"Look for the closest path to any exit," Sugarcane heaved as she got up beside me.

"No need," came a voice. Total dread fixed me in place as Sky's voice sounded from behind, and I turned to find him stepping out behind the corner, holding my textbook. "She went over there, by the way. Always a good idea to check behind you."

Now that I looked, I could see a series of light footsteps along the wall of the building.

"Follow me," Sky said.

We followed him in silence to a signature black MSS car parked outside of the complex. He opened the back door, and shut it behind us after making sure we both put on our seatbelts. He then got into the driver's seat himself, and started for school.

It was a short ride, and even though I knew the glass was tinted, I still felt the gazes of the pedestrians and other cars burning into me, thinking I was a criminal for sitting in the back seat of a car like this. Sugarcane sat on the other side; I could see her reflection in my window but didn't dare turn my head to look at her.

By the time we pulled into the parking lot, I felt like vomiting. I let my guard down and *that* was how she got hold of my textbook. The very textbook Sky warned us to never let fall into the wrong hands.
